Some of My Graphic Work

Meal Match

Problem

Busy students and early-career professionals struggle with meal planning due to time constraints and decision fatigue. Existing recipe platforms require manual entry, creating friction and leading to default takeout.

Objective

Design a low-effort, intuitive solution that reduces input friction and decision fatigue.

User Pain Points
  • “I don’t know what to cook with what I have.”
  • Too many steps to find a recipe
  • Time pressure after school/work
  • Overwhelming recipe options
  • Food expiring due to lack of planning
Solution

Users upload a single photo of their fridge. The system detects ingredients and generates quick meal suggestions, minimizing friction and cognitive load.

Canadian NPAS Redesign

Problem

Emergency alert systems must communicate critical information instantly. Existing NPAS interface presents dense info with limited hierarchy, making comprehension harder in stressful situations.

Objective

Modernize the alert interface to improve clarity, accessibility, and response efficiency.

User Pain Points
  • Difficulty identifying urgent info
  • Overwhelming text blocks
  • Limited accessibility considerations
  • Unclear action steps
Solution

Redesign introduces stronger hierarchy, simplified messaging, clear urgency indicators, and accessibility improvements to reduce cognitive load.
